\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

IOR targets the empty cell between IRL-based alignment auditing, which operates at
the model-weight level, and agent red-teaming, which probes fixed attack catalogues.
Its proposed contribution is the closed inference-probe feedback loop, in which each
round of probing refines the inferred objective and that objective targets the next
round; we present this as a design whose benefit remains to be demonstrated, since
the present evaluation does not yet close the loop. What we do establish is a working
system and an open, reproducible gym: the Goal Decomposition Judge makes objective
inference applicable to any black-box agent with a natural-language declared purpose,
the pluggable feature basis answers the dependence on an external model with a
deterministic floor and an agreement-quantified ensemble, and a first measurement
shows both that a model-free basis predicts held-out behaviour above baseline and
that, under fast mode, the IRL point estimate is largely reproducible by a
column-mean, which locates the method's value in uncertainty and full-mode
counterfactual reasoning. Honest accounting of these boundaries, including objectives
orthogonal to the declared purpose that the basis cannot see, is what separates the
present contribution, a credible system and protocol, from the stronger empirical
claims that completing the judge, ensemble, and closed-loop evaluations would
support. That completion, alongside provider-specific log adapters and multi-agent
inference, is the immediate future work.
